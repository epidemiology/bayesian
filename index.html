Resources

General Bayesian background

  • Bayesian Data Analysis by Gelman et al. (textbook)

    This is a general Bayesian data analysis textbook. It's not specific to epidemiology, but it's widely cited in the literature.

Epidemiology-specific resources

  • Chapter 24 in Modern Epidemiology by Rothman et al. (textbook)

    This chapter on Bayesian statistics for epidemiology contains much of the same content as the Greenland articles (see below).

  • Chapter 24 in Methods in Epidemiological Research by Dohoo et al. (textbook)

    This chapter in MER talks mostly about advanced methods, but it has a good summary of the difference between Bayesian and Frequentist approaches at the beginning, as well as a good description of MCMC models.

  • Bayesian perspectives for epidemiological research by Greenland (series of journal articles):

    1. Foundations and basic methods
    2. Regression analysis
    3. Bias analysis via missing-data methods

  • Bayesian Methods in Epidemiology by Broemeling (textbook, will be published August 2013).

Markov Chain Monte Carlo modeling

  • Hamra, G., MacLehose, R., & Richardson, D. (2013). Markov Chain Monte Carlo: an introduction for epidemiologists. International Journal of Epidemiology, 42(2), 627–634.

    This paper presents an overview of MCMC and how epidemiologists are using it and ways to implement in future studies.

  • Robert, C., & Casella, G. (2011). A Short History of Markov Chain Monte Carlo: Subjective Recollections from Incomplete Data. Statistical Science, 26(1), 102–115. (download PDF)

  • Kypraios, T., O'Neill, P. D., Huang, S. S., Rifas-Shiman, S. L., & Cooper, B. S. (2010). Assessing the role of undetected colonization and isolation precautions in reducing Methicillin-Resistant Staphylococcus aureus transmission in intensive care units. BMC Infectious Diseases, 10(1), 29.

    This is the paper discussed in the presentation.

    The following article describes the modeling used in Kypraios' paper:

    Forrester, M., Pettitt, A., & Gibson, G. (2006). Bayesian inference of hospital-acquired infectious diseases and control measures given imperfect surveillance data. Biostatistics, 8(2), 383–401.
